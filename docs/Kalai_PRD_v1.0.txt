Autonomous AI Marketing Agent for Indian SMBs


1. Executive Summary
Kalai (kalai.io) — from the Tamil word கலை meaning 'Art' — is an autonomous AI marketing agent built for Indian small and medium business owners who are not marketers. It handles the entire digital marketing lifecycle: brand creation, content generation, multi-platform publishing, engagement automation, lead scoring, and ROI tracking — without requiring the user to possess any marketing knowledge.

The product is born from a single observation: 82–95% of Indian SMBs operate with no systematic, paid, software-driven social media strategy. The incumbent tools (Hootsuite, Predis, Ocoya, Zoho Social) are fundamentally copilots — they require a skilled human to operate them. Kalai is the first autonomous agent in this category. The user describes their business once. Kalai builds their brand identity, creates their content calendar, publishes across platforms, responds to engagement, and reports business outcomes — all without requiring daily human input.

3. Feature Requirements
3.1 Feature Modules Overview

3.2 Module 1: Onboarding Conversation (V1)
The onboarding is not a form. It is a structured conversational flow powered by Claude Sonnet. The AI asks specific questions that extract the brand story, visual identity, content strategy, and campaign goals — without the user needing any marketing knowledge.

3.2.1 Conversation Flow
Business Introduction: "Tell me your business name and what you do in 2–3 sentences."
Work Evidence: "Show me 3 projects or pieces of work you're most proud of. Upload photos or links."
Best Client Story: "Describe your best client. What made them great to work with?"
Differentiation: "What's the one thing you wish every potential client understood about your work before contacting you?"
Goal Setting: "What's your #1 business goal for the next 90 days? (More leads / Build reputation / Launch new service / Grow followers)"
Platform Connection: "Connect your Instagram, LinkedIn, and Google Business Profile. We'll guide you if you don't have a Business account."

3.2.2 Outputs Generated from Onboarding
Brand Brain (stored in pgvector): voice model, visual DNA, audience profile, goal state
Brand Kit: Primary color, secondary color, accent color, typography recommendation
Logo variants: 3 options generated via Nano Banana Pro (Gemini 3 Image API)
Content pillars: 5 topic categories derived from the business story
30-day content calendar: Posts drafted, visuals generated, optimal timing assigned
First campaign brief: Tied to the stated 90-day goal

3.2.3 Technical Requirements
Total onboarding time: Under 10 minutes on a mobile device
Image upload: Support JPEG, PNG, HEIC up to 10MB per image
Instagram Business Account check: If personal account detected, provide guided conversion walkthrough
Facebook Page link check: Required for Instagram API — guided setup if missing
Offline resilience: Save progress to Supabase at each step, allow resume on different device

3.3 Module 2: Brand Genesis (V1)
3.3.1 Logo Generation
Model: Nano Banana Pro via Gemini API (gemini-3-pro-image-preview)
Prompt strategy: Business name + industry + brand adjectives → 3 logo variants (wordmark, icon, combination mark)
Output: PNG with transparent background, minimum 1024×1024px
User control: Regenerate any variant, approve one, mark others as alternatives
Storage: Cloudflare R2 — permanent storage tied to brand profile

3.3.2 Brand Color System
Primary color: Generated from industry + adjectives (architecture → slate blues; restaurants → warm earth tones)
Secondary + accent: Complementary palette generated by Claude
Output: Hex codes, RGB values, usage guidelines
Tamil/regional awareness: Colors culturally appropriate for Indian context

3.3.3 Brand Voice Model
Extracted from: Answers to onboarding questions, tone of uploaded project descriptions
Stored as: Vector embedding in Supabase pgvector
Used by: Every Claude API call for post copy generation — queried at call time
Tone spectrum: Professional / Warm-Professional / Conversational / Playful (user-selectable override)

3.4 Module 3: Content Factory (V1)
3.4.1 Post Generation Pipeline
Input: Campaign brief OR content pillar OR user-entered topic → Output: Platform-optimized post set


3.4.2 Visual Generation
Primary model: Nano Banana Pro (Gemini 3 Pro Image) — best for Tamil/Hindi text in images, Indian architectural content, Indian food photography
Fallback model: Flux Dev via Replicate — for non-text, purely photorealistic visuals
Brand consistency: Every generated image queries the Brand Brain for colors, style preferences, previous visual patterns
Text in image: Tamil and Hindi text rendered correctly — platform-appropriate typography
Watermark: Free tier images carry subtle "Powered by Kalai" badge — removable on paid plans

3.4.3 Copy Generation
Model: Claude Sonnet 4.6 (claude-sonnet-4-6)
Context injected: Brand voice embedding from pgvector, content pillar, campaign goal, platform, recent performance data
Quality controls: No generic phrases, no emojis unless user-approved, correct industry terminology
Hashtag generation: Platform-appropriate, niche-specific, mixed popularity tiers
Languages: English primary, Tamil/Hindi support for local market posts

3.5 Module 4: Multi-Platform Publisher (V1)
3.5.1 Platform Integrations

3.5.2 Publishing Engine
Job queue: Inngest (serverless) — no BullMQ, no Railway workers
Scheduling precision: Minute-level scheduling via Inngest delayed functions
Failure handling: Auto-retry 3 times with exponential backoff, alert user after final failure
Rate limiting: Per-platform rate tracking stored in Supabase, pre-publish limit checks
Instagram 25/day guard: Counter resets at midnight UTC, queues overflow to next day
Approval flow: Every post passes through user approval before publishing (V1). Autonomous mode adds one-tap mobile approval (V2).

3.6 Module 5: Smart Scheduler (V1)
Optimal posting time: Calculated per platform per account using engagement history (first 30 days uses industry defaults)
Content calendar: Visual week/month view, drag-drop to reschedule
Campaign cadence: Automated — based on campaign goals. "Get 100 leads" → daily posts for 30 days. "Build brand" → 3x/week.
Evergreen recycling: High-performing posts automatically queued for re-use at 90-day interval

3.7 Module 6: Autonomous Mode (V2)
Autonomous Mode is the product's defining feature. One toggle. Kalai prepares all content, posts at optimal times, responds to engagement — without the user needing to log in daily.


Notification: Push notification to mobile app → user taps Approve or Reschedule
Auto-approve timer: Optional — if user does not respond in X hours, post automatically publishes (V2, user-configurable)
DM agent: Reads all incoming DMs, generates brand-voice reply, sends after user approval OR auto-sends for FAQ-type messages
Comment agent: Reads new comments, responds to questions and compliments, flags negative sentiment for human review
Lead escalation: High-intent signals ("how much does this cost?") immediately notified to user regardless of auto-approve setting

3.8 Module 7: AI Engagement Agent (V2)
3.8.1 DM Classification System

3.8.2 Lead Scoring Model
Lead scores are calculated from signals available through Meta's API — no per-user behavioral tracking that violates platform terms.
Signal: DM sent (+40 points)
Signal: Pricing question asked (+30 points)
Signal: Comment with buying intent (+25 points)
Signal: Multiple comments in 7 days (+20 points)
Signal: Bio link click via tracked URL (+35 points)
Signal: Story reply (+15 points)
Decay: Score decays 10% per week of inactivity
Threshold: Score >70 = hot lead, notified immediately. Score 40–70 = warm, shown in weekly digest.

4.1 Performance

4.2 Scalability
Architecture: Serverless-first (Vercel + Inngest) — scales to 0 at night, handles traffic spikes automatically
Database: Supabase PostgreSQL with connection pooling via PgBouncer
Storage: Cloudflare R2 — unlimited, zero egress cost
AI cost scaling: Per-user AI cost estimated at ₹50–150/month. Pricing model maintains 70%+ gross margin even at scale.
Target: Handle 10,000 active users without architecture changes

4.3 Reliability
Uptime target: 99.5% monthly (Vercel SLA)
Post failure alerting: Sentry captures all failed publish jobs within 60 seconds
Data backup: Supabase daily automated backups, 30-day retention
Graceful degradation: If AI generation fails, fall back to previously approved content templates

4.4 Security
Authentication: Supabase Auth with Google OAuth, magic links, email/password
API keys: All third-party keys stored in Vercel environment variables, never in client code
Social tokens: OAuth tokens stored encrypted in Supabase, row-level security per workspace
Multi-tenancy isolation: Supabase RLS policies ensure complete data isolation between workspaces
HTTPS: Enforced everywhere. No HTTP in production.

4.5 Mobile Requirements
Platform: iOS 15+ and Android 8.0+ (API level 26+)
Budget phone support: Fully functional on 3GB RAM Android devices
Offline: View existing content calendar and analytics without internet connection
Push notifications: Expo Notifications for post approval alerts, lead notifications, Sunday digest
PWA: Next.js web app is mobile-responsive from V1 launch — serves as V1 mobile experience before native app ships


5. Pricing & Monetisation Strategy
5.1 Pricing Tiers

5.2 Pricing Psychology (India-Specific)
Annual billing primary: Bypasses monthly subscription fatigue — the #1 psychological barrier for Indian SMBs
Left-digit effect: ₹9,999 not ₹10,000. ₹29,999 not ₹30,000.
Free forever > 14-day trial: Indian B2B decisions take longer than 14 days. Free forever converts better.
GST-inclusive display: Show ₹9,999 as ₹9,999 incl. GST — not ₹8,474 + GST which causes checkout shock
UPI first: Razorpay integration. UPI shown before cards. Never show USD pricing to Indian users.

8. Constraints, Assumptions & Risks
8.1 Technical Constraints
Instagram API: 25 posts per user per 24 hours. Design scheduler to never approach this limit.
Instagram DM API: Requires Meta Advanced Access review. Apply on Day 1 of development.
Instagram Business Account mandatory: Personal accounts cannot use Graph API. Onboarding must detect and guide conversion.
WhatsApp posting: No API exists for scheduling posts to WhatsApp profiles. Removed from scope permanently.
Koo integration: No official API. Removed from scope permanently.
Background tasks (mobile): Expo background tasks run on OS-controlled schedule. Minimum ~15 minutes on Android. Acceptable for Kalai's use case.

8.2 Business Risks & Mitigations

8.3 Key Assumptions
Users have or can be guided to create Instagram Business accounts linked to Facebook Pages.
Users can provide at least 3 photos of their work during onboarding (quality of output depends on input quality).
Google Business Profile API remains free and stable through 2026–2027.
Indian SMBs will accept annual billing model when presented with clear value and UPI payment.
Meta Advanced Access review takes 1–3 days as per current 2025 reports.
